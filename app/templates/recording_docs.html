Video Recording Guidelines

Face Authenticity Dataset Collection

Purpose

These recordings support the training and evaluation of a face authenticity (deepfake detection) model. High-quality, diverse inputs improve the model's robustness across real-world conditions.

Before recording, you'll be asked to sign up/login. This is to only capture user_id and session_id for easier identification.

Data Handling, Privacy & Ethics

We take privacy and responsible data use seriously.

  • No raw webcam video is stored.
  • All processing occurs in-memory during recording.
  • Only derived signals and features are retained, such as:
    • Facial landmarks and geometry
    • Temporal motion patterns
    • Texture and consistency signals used for authenticity analysis
  • No audio, background imagery, or identifiable video frames are saved.
  • Record only yourself or faces you are explicitly authorized to use.
  • Do not speak or display personal, sensitive, or identifying information.

Visual Verification

During recording, the interface provides a visual confirmation that only features are being extracted and that no video is stored.

Example Verification Screenshot:

The interface should indicate that processing is live, feature-based, and that no webcam footage is saved.

General Recording Rules

  • 1 Each recording lasts 45 seconds (automatically handled).
  • 2 Use a front-facing webcam.
  • 3 Disable all filters, beauty modes, or camera enhancements.
  • 4 Ensure your face is clearly visible and in focus.
  • 5 Record multiple samples across different conditions.

REAL (Authentic) Recordings

  • • You must be physically present in front of the webcam.
  • • Only one face should appear in the frame.
  • • Blink naturally and maintain a neutral expression.
  • • Small, natural head movements are allowed.
  • •
    Vary conditions across recordings:
    • – Lighting (bright, dim, side-lit)
    • – Distance from camera
    • – Slight angle changes
    • – Different non-sensitive backgrounds

FAKE (Non-Authentic) Recordings

  • • Screen playback of a face video.
  • • Printed photographs presented to the webcam.
  • • Pre-recorded deepfake videos.
  • • Live face manipulation tools used strictly for research and testing.

Examples of real-time face manipulation tools suitable for experimentation (use responsibly and only with permitted content):

  • → DeepLiveCam
  • → Any equivalent live face reenactment or swap tool you already have access to

Do NOT

  • ✗ Change lighting mid-recording.
  • ✗ Move or reposition the camera.
  • ✗ Wear hats, masks, sunglasses, or face coverings.
  • ✗ Enable AR effects or virtual backgrounds.

Final Notes

The objective is diversity and realism, not perfection. Recording across varied conditions significantly improves model generalization.

When unsure whether a scenario is useful, record it — filtering happens later.
